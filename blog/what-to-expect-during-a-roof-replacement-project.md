# What to Expect During a Roof Replacement Project in South Florida

**The complete day-by-day guide to your roof replacement — from permit to final inspection**

---

## TL;DR

**A typical residential roof replacement in South Florida takes 1-3 days for shingle roofs, 3-5 days for tile, and 5-7 days for metal roofs.** The process includes: permit acquisition by your contractor (1-2 weeks before work), day 1 tear-off and deck inspection, day 2 underlayment and material installation, day 3 cleanup and final details, followed by building department inspections. In HVHZ areas (all of Broward and Palm Beach Counties), inspectors verify enhanced fastening patterns, proper underlayment installation, and wind mitigation specifications. Expect significant noise during tear-off (sounds like constant hammering), debris protection tarps around your home, dumpster placement in driveway, and daily cleanup. Weather delays are common during Florida's rainy season (May-October) and can add 1-3 days to timeline. Before work begins, move outdoor furniture, secure pets indoors, park vehicles away from house, notify neighbors, and confirm insurance claim approval if applicable. Professional contractors handle all permits, inspections, and coordination — you should never be surprised by the process.

## The Pre-Construction Phase: Permits and Scheduling

**Before a single shingle is removed, several things must happen.**

### Permit Acquisition (1-2 Weeks Before Work)

**In Broward and Palm Beach Counties, all roof replacements require building permits.**

Your contractor should handle:

- **Permit application** — Submitted with plans, photos, engineering (if required)
- **Permit fees** — Typically $300-$800 depending on home size
- **Engineering review** — Some jurisdictions require stamped engineering for HVHZ
- **HOA approval** — If applicable, submitted simultaneously
- **Insurance coordination** — If claim-related, approval before work begins

**Red flag: Contractors who suggest skipping permits to "save money" are putting your insurance, resale value, and safety at risk.**

### Material Ordering (1-2 Weeks Before)

Once permit is approved:

- Roofing materials ordered (shingles, tiles, metal panels)
- Underlayment and supplies delivered to warehouse
- Dumpster scheduled for delivery
- Crew assigned and schedule confirmed

### Final Pre-Work Steps (1 Week Before)

**Your contractor should:**

✓ Confirm start date and timeline

✓ Provide written schedule with inspection dates

✓ Explain daily activities and expectations

✓ Provide contact numbers for site supervisor

✓ Discuss weather contingency plans

**You should:**

✓ Move outdoor furniture away from house

✓ Remove items hanging on interior walls (vibration can knock pictures down)

✓ Cover items in attic with tarps (dust will fall through)

✓ Arrange for pets to stay indoors or elsewhere

✓ Notify neighbors about noise and activity

✓ Clear driveway for dumpster placement

## Day 1: Tear-Off and Deck Inspection

**This is the noisiest, messiest day of the project.**

### Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Dumpster delivery and property protection:**

- Dumpster placed in driveway or street (permit required for street placement)
- Tarps and plywood laid around house perimeter
- Gutters covered or removed temporarily
- HVAC units and outdoor equipment protected

**Tear-off begins:**

- Workers remove existing shingles, tiles, or metal panels
- Old underlayment and felt paper removed
- Debris thrown directly into dumpster
- Roof deck exposed for inspection

**What it sounds like:** Constant scraping, tearing, and impact sounds. Like someone is walking on your ceiling with steel boots while ripping up flooring. It's LOUD.

**What it looks like:** Workers on your roof, debris flying, dust in the air. Your yard will look like a construction site.

### Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

**Deck inspection and repairs:**

- Building inspector arrives to inspect roof deck (first required inspection)
- Contractor identifies any rotted or damaged plywood/OSB
- Damaged decking cut out and replaced
- New deck sections secured with proper fastening
- Drip edge installed at eaves and rakes

**Critical HVHZ requirement:** Deck must be solid, properly fastened, and meet current code. Inspectors check fastener spacing and deck thickness.

**What happens if rain comes:** Contractors tarp the exposed deck immediately. Modern synthetic underlayments are often partially installed same-day to provide weather protection.

### End of Day 1

**Your roof will be:**

- Completely stripped to deck
- Inspected and approved by building department
- Tarped or partially underlaymened for weather protection
- Cleaned of major debris (though dust and small pieces remain)

**Your home will be:**

- Dusty (ceiling dust, attic dust)
- Noisy all day
- Protected from weather

**You should:** Keep pets and children indoors. Don't park under roof edges. Avoid walking near house perimeter.

## Day 2: Underlayment and Material Installation

**This is when your new roof starts taking shape.**

### Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Underlayment installation:**

- **Self-adhering underlayment** installed in valleys, eaves, and critical areas
- **Synthetic underlayment** installed across main roof deck
- All seams overlapped per manufacturer specifications
- Building inspector arrives for underlayment inspection (second required inspection)

**HVHZ Critical:** Underlayment must be:

- Properly overlapped (minimum 6" side laps, 4" end laps)
- Fastened per code (staples or nails at specified spacing)
- Sealed at all penetrations (vents, pipes, chimneys)
- Approved for HVHZ use (not all underlayments qualify)

**Inspector checks:** Fastening pattern, overlap distances, penetration sealing, proper coverage.

### Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

**Roofing material installation begins:**

**For shingle roofs:**

- Starter strip installed at eaves
- First course of shingles installed
- Workers progress up roof from eaves to ridge
- Enhanced fastening pattern used (6-8 nails per shingle in HVHZ)

**For tile roofs:**

- Battens installed (if applicable)
- Tiles secured with foam adhesive + mechanical fasteners
- Hip and ridge tiles installed with enhanced fastening
- Each tile individually secured per HVHZ requirements

**For metal roofs:**

- Clips or fastening system installed
- Metal panels placed and secured
- Seams mechanically seamed or locked
- Trim and flashing installed

**What it sounds like:** Nail guns firing constantly (sounds like rapid-fire hammering). Less loud than tear-off but still significant noise.

### End of Day 2

**Your roof will be:**

- 40-70% complete (varies by roof complexity)
- Weather-tight from completed sections
- Actively being worked on

**For simple roofs:** Day 2 might see completion of all roofing material.

**For complex roofs:** Material installation continues into Day 3.

---

## Day 3: Completion and Final Details

**The final push to completion.**

### Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Finishing roofing material:**

- Remaining shingles, tiles, or metal panels installed
- Ridge cap installed at peaks and hips
- All penetrations properly flashed and sealed
- Pipe boots, vent flashings, and chimney flashings installed

**HVHZ nailing inspection:**

- Inspector arrives to check fastening patterns (third required inspection)
- Inspects every section for proper nail placement
- Verifies nail count and spacing
- Checks exposed nail heads in metal roofs

**What inspector looks for:**

- **Shingles:** 6-8 nails per shingle (vs 4 in non-HVHZ areas)
- **Tiles:** Foam adhesive coverage + mechanical fasteners
- **Metal:** Proper clip spacing and fastener schedule
- **All:** No missed areas, no improper fastening

### Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

**Site cleanup and final details:**

- Gutters reinstalled or uncovered
- Magnetic sweep of property for nails
- Dumpster removal scheduled
- Property walkthrough with homeowner
- Final inspection scheduled with building department

**Cleanup includes:**

✓ Remove all tarps and protection materials

✓ Blow off roof surface

✓ Clean gutters of debris

✓ Sweep driveway and walkways

✓ Check yard for debris

✓ Magnetic sweep (picks up 99% of nails)

### End of Day 3

**Your roof will be:**

- 100% complete
- Weather-tight and fully functional
- Cleaned and ready for final inspection

**You should:** Walk property with contractor, ask questions, confirm satisfaction before final payment.

## Day 4-5: Final Inspection and Approval

**The building department returns for final approval.**

### Final Building Inspection

**Inspector verifies:**

✓ All work completed per approved plans

✓ Proper fastening throughout

✓ Correct materials used

✓ Flashing properly installed

✓ Ventilation adequate

✓ No visible defects

**If inspection passes:**

- Building department issues "Certificate of Completion"
- Your permit is closed
- Roof is officially code-compliant
- Insurance company can be notified of completion

**If inspection fails:**

- Inspector provides written list of deficiencies
- Contractor must correct issues
- Re-inspection scheduled (usually 1-3 days later)
- No additional permit fees for re-inspection

**Common failure reasons:**

- Missed nails or improper fastening
- Incorrect flashing details
- Missing underlayment in specific areas
- Ventilation inadequate

**Professional contractors rarely fail final inspections** — they know code requirements and install correctly the first time.

## Timeline by Roof Type

**How long will YOUR roof take?**

### Asphalt Shingle Roofs

**Typical timeline: 1-3 days**

- **Day 1:** Tear-off, deck inspection
- **Day 2:** Underlayment, shingle installation
- **Day 3:** Completion, cleanup (if needed)

**Simple roofs (gable, hip) with no complications:** 1-2 days

**Complex roofs (multiple stories, valleys, penetrations):** 2-3 days

### Tile Roofs

**Typical timeline: 3-5 days**

- **Day 1:** Tear-off, deck inspection
- **Day 2:** Underlayment, batten installation
- **Day 3-4:** Tile installation (slower than shingles)
- **Day 5:** Completion, cleanup

**Why longer:** Each tile must be individually placed and secured with foam adhesive + fasteners. Much slower than nailing shingles.

### Metal Roofs

**Typical timeline: 3-7 days**

- **Day 1:** Tear-off, deck inspection
- **Day 2:** Underlayment, clip system installation
- **Day 3-5:** Panel placement and seaming
- **Day 6-7:** Trim, flashing, completion

**Why longer:** Metal panels require precise measurement, cutting, and mechanical seaming. Standing seam roofs take longer than screw-down panels.

### Flat Roofs

**Typical timeline: 2-4 days**

- **Day 1:** Tear-off, deck inspection
- **Day 2:** Insulation, TPO/PVC membrane installation
- **Day 3-4:** Seam welding, flashing, completion

## Weather Delays in Florida's Rainy Season

**Rain is inevitable in South Florida — plan for it.**

### May - October: Rainy Season

**Expect afternoon thunderstorms:**

- Typically 1:00-5:00 PM
- Can halt work for 1-3 hours
- May delay timeline by 1-2 days total

**Contractor protocol:**

- Stop work when rain begins
- Tarp exposed areas immediately
- Wait for roof to dry before resuming
- May work extended hours after rain passes

### Hurricane Season Considerations

**June 1 - November 30:**

- Contractors monitor forecasts closely
- Work may be paused 3-5 days before storm
- Roof secured with emergency tarps if incomplete
- Timeline extended by weather delay

**If hurricane approaches mid-project:**

- Roof will be tarped and secured
- Work pauses until after storm
- Post-storm damage assessment
- Work resumes when safe

### Winter: Best Weather Window

**November - April:**

- Lowest rainfall
- More predictable timelines
- Less weather-related delays
- Higher contractor demand

**Pro tip:** Schedule roof replacements in winter months if possible — shorter timelines and fewer weather surprises.

## HVHZ Inspection Requirements: What Makes South Florida Different

**Every roof in Broward and Palm Beach Counties must meet 180mph wind standards.**

### Required Inspections

**1. Deck Inspection (Day 1)**

Inspector verifies:

- Deck thickness (minimum 7/16" OSB or 1/2" plywood)
- Fastener spacing (6" edges, 12" field)
- Solid deck with no gaps
- Proper deck attachment to trusses/rafters

**2. Underlayment Inspection (Day 2)**

Inspector verifies:

- HVHZ-approved underlayment used
- Proper overlap (6" side, 4" end)
- Fastening pattern (staples or nails every 12")
- All penetrations sealed
- Valleys double-layered

**3. Nailing Inspection (Day 2-3)**

Inspector verifies:

- Enhanced fastening (6-8 nails per shingle)
- Nail placement in "nailing zone"
- Perimeter enhanced fastening (3 rows)
- Hip/ridge enhanced fastening
- No missed areas

**4. Final Inspection (Day 4-5)**

Inspector verifies:

- All work complete per plans
- Proper flashing at all penetrations
- Adequate ventilation
- No visible defects
- Certificate of Completion issued

### Wind Mitigation Form OIR-B1-1802

**Your contractor should provide:**

- Completed wind mitigation form
- Roof-to-wall connection documentation
- Secondary water resistance documentation
- Opening protection documentation (if applicable)

**Value:** Submit to insurance company for discounts of 10-45% on premiums.

**Roof replacement is stressful for everyone — including your pets.**

### Pets

**Best option:** Board pets or have them stay elsewhere during work days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does a roof replacement take in South Florida?

A typical residential roof replacement in South Florida takes 1-3 days for asphalt shingle roofs, 3-5 days for tile roofs, and 3-7 days for metal roofs. The timeline includes tear-off (Day 1), underlayment and material installation (Days 2-3), and cleanup/final details (Day 3). However, weather delays are common during Florida's rainy season (May-October) and can add 1-3 days to the schedule. Complex roofs with multiple stories, valleys, or unusual features take longer. Final building inspection typically occurs 1-2 days after completion, bringing total timeline to approximately 1 week from start to permit closure. The permit acquisition process takes an additional 1-2 weeks before work can begin.

### What are the permit requirements for roof replacement in Broward County?

All roof replacements in Broward County require building permits, which your contractor should handle. The permit process includes: application submission with plans and photos ($300-$800 fee), engineering review for HVHZ compliance (may require stamped engineering), and approval before work begins. During the project, building inspectors conduct multiple required inspections: deck inspection after tear-off (verifies proper decking and fastening), underlayment inspection (verifies HVHZ-approved materials and proper installation), nailing inspection (verifies enhanced fastening patterns with 6-8 nails per shingle), and final inspection (comprehensive review before Certificate of Completion). Attempting roof replacement without permits is illegal, voids your homeowners insurance, creates resale problems, and eliminates wind mitigation insurance discounts. Professional contractors include all permitting and inspection coordination in their service.

### How loud is a roof replacement and how can I prepare?

Roof replacement is extremely loud, especially during tear-off on Day 1, which sounds like constant demolition directly above your head and makes phone calls or video meetings impossible. Nail gun installation (Days 2-3) is also very loud with rhythmic hammering sounds throughout the day. Work typically starts at 7:00-8:00 AM and continues until 5:00-6:00 PM. The entire house vibrates during work, which can knock pictures off walls, shift items on shelves, and create significant dust. To prepare: move outdoor furniture 10+ feet from house, remove wall decorations from rooms below the roof, cover attic belongings with tarps, keep pets indoors in quietest room or board them elsewhere during work days, close windows and use white noise to reduce stress for pets, park vehicles away from driveway, and plan to be away from home if possible during tear-off day. Notify neighbors 24-48 hours in advance about the noise.

### What happens if it rains during my roof replacement?

Rain delays are common during Florida's rainy season (May-October) when afternoon thunderstorms typically occur between 1:00-5:00 PM. When rain begins, professional contractors immediately stop work and tarp all exposed areas to protect your home. Work resumes once the roof surface dries, which may take 1-3 hours after rain stops. Rain delays typically add 1-2 days to overall project timeline. Contractors may work extended hours after rain passes to stay on schedule. During hurricane season (June-November), contractors monitor forecasts closely and may pause work 3-5 days before a storm, securing the roof with emergency tarps if incomplete. Your contract should specify that you're not charged extra for weather delays. To minimize rain risk, schedule roof replacements during Florida's dry season (November-April) when weather is more predictable and timelines are more reliable.

### What questions should I ask my roofing contractor before work begins?

Before roof replacement begins, ask your contractor these critical questions: 1) What's the exact day-by-day timeline for my roof, including all inspection points? 2) Who will be the on-site supervisor and what's their direct phone number? 3) How will you protect my property during work (tarps, dumpster placement, landscaping protection)? 4) Will you pull all required permits and what's the inspection schedule? 5) Exactly what materials are you using and are they HVHZ-rated? 6) What warranties will I receive (manufacturer and workmanship) and will I get written copies? 7) Will you provide completed wind mitigation form OIR-B1-1802 for insurance discounts? 8) What's the payment schedule (never pay 100% upfront)? 9) What are potential additional costs like rotted decking replacement (get per-unit pricing)? 10) What happens if it rains or we fail an inspection? Get all answers in writing before signing contract.
